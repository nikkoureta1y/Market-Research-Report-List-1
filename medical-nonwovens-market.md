<p><p>The medical nonwovens market comprises various production technologies including drylaid, airlaid, wetlaid, and spunlaid processes. Drylaid involves carding fibers to create a web, while airlaid uses an air stream to distribute fibers evenly. Wetlaid, similar to traditional papermaking, utilizes a slurry of fibers and water to form sheets. Spunlaid combines melting polymer extrusions and blowing them onto a conveyor, producing strong and versatile materials. Together, these methods cater to diverse medical applications, including hygiene products and surgical fabrics.</p></p>
