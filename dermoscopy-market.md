<p><p>The dermoscopy market is primarily categorized into traditional and digital dermatoscopes. Traditional dermatoscopes utilize optical magnification and polarized light to enhance skin lesion visualization, allowing dermatologists to diagnose conditions manually. In contrast, digital dermatoscopes incorporate advanced imaging technology, enabling high-resolution capture and storage of images for analysis. Digital systems often include features like teledermatology capabilities and automated algorithms for improved accuracy. Both types play crucial roles in skin cancer detection and overall dermatological assessment, catering to varying clinical needs.</p></p>
