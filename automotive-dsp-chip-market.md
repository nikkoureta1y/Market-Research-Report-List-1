<p><p>The automotive DSP chip market is divided into single-core and multi-core DSP types. Single-core DSPs feature one processing unit, suitable for simpler tasks like basic sound processing and vehicle monitoring systems. In contrast, multi-core DSPs incorporate multiple processing units, allowing for more complex computations and parallel processing, essential for advanced applications like real-time sensor fusion, infotainment systems, and autonomous driving algorithms. The choice between single-core and multi-core typically hinges on performance requirements and application complexity.</p></p>
