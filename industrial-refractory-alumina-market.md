<p><p>The industrial refractory alumina market encompasses several types of alumina materials, each serving specific applications. Calcined alumina is produced by heating aluminum hydroxide, offering high thermal stability and low reactivity, making it suitable for high-temperature applications. Fused alumina is created by melting alumina, resulting in a dense material with excellent wear resistance. Tabular alumina is sintered at high temperatures, featuring a unique crystalline structure ideal for refractory linings. Other types may include specialty grades tailored for unique industrial needs.</p></p>
